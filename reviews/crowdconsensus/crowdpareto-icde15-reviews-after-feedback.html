Title Crowdsourcing Pareto-Optimal Object Finding by Pairwise Comparisons
Masked Reviewer ID: Assigned_Reviewer_1
Review:
Question 
Overall Recommendation Weak Reject
Summary of the paper (what is being proposed and in what context; brief justification of your overall recommendation). One paragraph This paper proposes a new method for finding Pareto-optimal objects with crowdsourcing. Toward this goal, it exploits pair-wise comparisons for identifying unknown relations of objects for each attribute and asks pair-wise questions to the crowds. In this process, this paper develops several heuristic algorithms for reducing the number of questions as many as possible. Experimental results show the proposed algorithms can significantly reduce the number of questions compared to the brute-force method.
Three (or more) strong points about the paper (Please be precise and explicit; clearly explain the value and nature of the contribution). (i) This paper is easy to understand.

(ii) The improvement gains in experimental results are significant.

(iii) Case studies in experiments are interesting.
Three (or more) weak points about the paper (Please indicate clearly whether the paper has any mistakes, missing related work, or results that cannot be considered a contribution; write it so that he authors can understand what is seen as negative aspects in their work). (i) The technical notions and terms used in this paper are unclear. The notions of indifference, incomparability, and undefined have different meanings, but the notions were not distinguished clearly.

(ii) The idea of the macro-ordering strategy is straightforward. The key ideas in heuristic algorithms only come from the micro-ordering strategy.

(iii) The proposed algorithm need to be validated more thoroughly over the synthetic datasets. It is based on NBA dataset, which follows the correlated distribution. Other datasets with various data distributions, independent and anti-correlated distribution, need be used for extensive evaluation.
Detailed Evaluation (Contribution, Pros/Cons, Errors); please number each point This paper uses the notion of indifference, meaning that two objects are equally preferred. It is different from incomparability, meaning that two objects do not dominate each other. If two objects are incomparable in some attribute U, they can also be incomparable in V (including U). From this viewpoint, if x and y are incomparable on {story}, they can be incomparable on {story, music, acting}.

- However, this paper uses confused notions between incomparability and indifference: in page 1, the notion of indifference is that two objects are equally preferred. Meanwhile, in page 5 (intransitivity of object dominance), the meaning of indifference is similar to incomparability. Without clear distinction between the two notions, the proposed algorithm does not produce any meaningful results.

- If this papers intends to use the notion of equal preferences for indifference, it may be possible to use bi-directed arrows.

- In addition, the preferences between objects are undefined, meaning no preference (Fig 2 in page 2) should distinguish from indifference and incomparability. Initially, the undefined preference can be inferred by the incomparability notion, i.e., they do not dominate each other, and the preference for undefined relation can be changed.

Question 
Overall Recommendation Clear Accept
Summary of the paper (what is being proposed and in what context; brief justification of your overall recommendation). One paragraph This paper addresses a variation of skyline query in which there are strict partial orders among objects in individual attributes. The technique has a wide variety of applications and the authors show that there is a good solution. The paper provides a nice formal framework and shows the results of an extensive set of experiments.
Three (or more) strong points about the paper (Please be precise and explicit; clearly explain the value and nature of the contribution). S1. The problem of finding Pareto-optimal objects with those attributes whose absolute values are difficult to define, has a wide range of applications.
S2. The authors gives a concise formal framework that defines a general framework for the problem.
S3. They conducted an extensive set of experiments with a variety of settings, and found that their heuristics can generate nearly optimal results.
Three (or more) weak points about the paper (Please indicate clearly whether the paper has any mistakes, missing related work, or results that cannot be considered a contribution; write it so that he authors can understand what is seen as negative aspects in their work). W1. The pure pair-wise approach is not necessarily practical. Therefore, it would be better to mention the possibility of hybrid approach that combine the value-based and pairwise comparisons.
W2. They focus only on a full sequential execution model that does not necessarily exploit the full potential of crowdsourcing.
W3. FRQ involves many heuristics and how each heuristic is effective is unclear.
Detailed Evaluation (Contribution, Pros/Cons, Errors); please number each point D1. Both rules for the contradiction prevention and resolution depend on the order in which two inconsistent results appeared. The effect is not discussed.
D2. In Section IV, Q^1_{can} appears suddenly.
D3. In Section V-A(2), the ratio of pairwise comparisons required by RandomQ to that used by BruteForce is 0.0048, which is the same as that used by FRQ. This is strange.
D4. In the last sentence of the same paragraph states that Fig 8 shows that the number grows linearly by both |C| and |O|. However, it does not seem true. In particular, the number does not grow linearly by |O|.
D5. Why did the authors choose different tasks for the case studies 1 and 2? At least the photo tasks can be performed in the same setting as that of the case study 1.

Overall Recommendation Weak Reject
Summary of the paper (what is being proposed and in what context; brief justification of your overall recommendation). One paragraph In this paper, the authors studied the problem of finding all Pareto-optimal objects by asking fewest possible questions to the crowd. The problem of aggregating partial to find the total order is not new. In addition, the proposed solutions are not novel and mainly based on heuristic. It is not clear whether the proposed the heuristic solution is good enough. In other words, there is no bound.
Three (or more) strong points about the paper (Please be precise and explicit; clearly explain the value and nature of the contribution). 1. An interesting problem.
2. A case study is conducted on the real platform
Three (or more) weak points about the paper (Please indicate clearly whether the paper has any mistakes, missing related work, or results that cannot be considered a contribution; write it so that he authors can understand what is seen as negative aspects in their work). 1. The authors did not prove the hardness of the proposed problem, I mean selecting the minimum number of questions for finding pareto optimal objects.

2. The proposed heuristic solutions do not have bound to the optimal solutions.

3. The experimental study is very limited.

4. The authors assumed that the workers are 100% correct, which is often not the case.
Detailed Evaluation (Contribution, Pros/Cons, Errors); please number each point As I mentioned above, the problem is not new. After all, the crowdsourcing-based skyline queries have been studied before. Further more, most of the solutions listed in the paper are heuristic-based. For the experimental study, the authors only used simulation to study the effectiveness of different parameters.
