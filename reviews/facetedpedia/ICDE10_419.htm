Major strong points of the paper (please explain clearly the value and nature of the contributions) 1. The paper is reasonably well written
2. Key notions are well explained
3. System implementation
Major weak points of the paper (please indicate clearly the perceived limitations of the paper, especially technical errors, missing related work and recycled results) 1. Non-convincing motivation
2. Dubious significance and originality
3. Unusable because of very poor performance
4. Unconvincing evaluation
Detailed comments The paper proposes to dynamically build topics categories (aka ontologies/schemas)on demand to obtain more accurate wikipedia articles which would in turn reflect the query intention. The idea is to use the first set of returned wikipedia articles as a conduit to build the initial set of facets (rooted hierarchies of wikipedia categories). These rely on hyperlinks in the target articles to build such facets. Many definitions (12 of them) are presented. Five algorithms dealing with discovering and building facets, their ranking, computing costs, and facet selection are presented and described. A user study focusing on the comparison with another system called Castanet is used to evaluate the proposed approach.

The paper is fairly well written and mostly well organized though it is sometimes hard to read because of the many definitions (12 of them) and the multitude of notations introduced. The focus is mainly on the querying of Wikipedia documents. The paper does not make any specific claims as to the generality of the proposed approach called, Facetedpedia. The issue of obtaining more accurate and relevant results has been researched extensively in the area of Information Retrieval. In this respect, it is not clear where the paper's originality lies.

The motivation is not convincing. The paper claims that with keyword search on wikipedia, one would typically have to go through a list of articles and then hyperlinks to find the article of interest. Surprisingly, there is not one single piece of verifiable evidence to substantiate the claim that is used to conduct this research. This is in my opinion a fatal flaw. One may anecdotally dispute this claim from personal experience. Even If this important aspect is overlooked, the approach itself suffers from many severe drawbacks. The idea of building facets/schemas/ontologies from data has received considerable attention in the respective research communities. The simple reason why solutions have not been widely deployed have to do with the exorbitant performance cost when facets are dynamically constructed on per-query basis. This is why static categorizations are mostly used in commercial products.

The example is ill-chosen. Typically PhD students use scientific venues to find information about their potential thesis. Wikipedia is mostly for "casual" inquiries. The paper makes a major assumption, and without evidence, that all hyperlinks in an article are evidence of strong relevance. This is a strong statement that needs to be substantiated.

The approach to discovering, building, and selecting facets is in my opinion an overkill, and has almost zero chance of being adopted in any real applications, let alone wikipedia. The approach defies reason when it assumes that users would pose keyword queries just to get articles that would then be used to construct facets which would then be used to find other articles that hopefully are different from the ones already found and of better quality! The authors themselves admit that this process would be difficult for the lay wikipedia user when they state on page before last one, first column, last paragraph " The users all have college degrees or are in college, including university students, faculty, staff, and financial, and IT company workers. **"We believe these users are experienced with Web search and comfortable with more sophisticated access mechanism.."** (our emphasis).

The technical part is largely based on definitions with no new insight or surprise. The cost definition is odd as it is based on the user's navigation patterns which may not even be available or worse are not the desired mode of interaction! The algorithms are also mostly expected. The part that was really ad-hoc and showed little understanding about the evaluation is the users study. The paper does not state what criteria were used to select the proposed set of queries. More importantly, the comparison with a limited-functionality system, Castanet, shows that there are no major differences in terms of the user ratings!

The paper would need to take a totally different approach to filtering if it is to make a useful contribution. I am not convinced the paper has done so in this paper.

Question 
Major strong points of the paper (please explain clearly the value and nature of the contributions) Easy to read paper.
Major weak points of the paper (please indicate clearly the perceived limitations of the paper, especially technical errors, missing related work and recycled results) There are some fundamental flaws in the paper about facets in Wikipedia. It assumes that facets already exist prior to queries. I doubt such assumption holds. With the assumption, the entire problem becomes very contrived. The paper has avoided mentioning how facets are derived both in the proposed solution and in the conducted experiment.
Detailed comments Actually, the main idea of the paper is simple and should be presented in a more straightforward manner without involving unnecessary definitions. The notion of safe reaching facet is really not required if relevant category hierarchy can be defined to remove categories that does not reach any target articles.

How do we know that the attribute articles of target articles are correct? The paper seems to avoid a lot of issues related to reliability of inter-category links and target-attribute articles links. In practice, the link information is noisy.

The user evaluation experiment should have used precision and recall as performance metrics to determine the effectiveness of the faceted query interface.

The right side of the 1st para of page 3 has been truncated.
